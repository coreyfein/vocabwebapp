Who is this for?

Readers! More specifically, readers who want to actually learn the words they look up.

I come across new words often -- reading the news, books, or even doing crossword puzzles -- and find that I often see words that I know I've looked up before months ago, but can't remember what they mean. The words that I actually remember tend to be ones that I discover and look up once, and then happen to see again soon after. ("Oh yeah! I just saw that word in the article I read yesterday about monarch butterflies!") This is a tool that aims to replicate that recognition for every word you discover as you read, even if you don't see it that often.

Adding words

Using it is simple. When you come across a word you don't know, add it to your list just by following a few quick steps. We'll handle retrieving the definition, synonyms, examples, and etymology. You can add just the word, the sentence where you discovered it, and/or a description of the work as a whole (e.g. "An article in the NYT about monarch butterflies"). Including all three is a uniquely effective method for ultimately learning a word's meaning, and Vocab for Readers makes the process simple.

See below for instructions on how to add a word from your phone or computer. If you already have a vocab list, you can upload it here.

Quizzes

Once you've added some words to your list, the next step is to quiz yourself. Each quiz contains 10-15 words, depending on how many you've added recently that you haven't been quizzed on yet. For each word in the quiz, think about whether you know what it means. Then, click a button to show the definition, synonyms, etc, and choose "Correct" if you got it right. Only mark it correct if you're confident that you really know it -- you should be able to use it in a sentence, not just have the definition memorized. We'll use your quiz results to help decide what to quiz you on next.

Soon, you'll be able to sign up for email reminders linking to your next quiz (and at some point, you'll be able to customize the frequency).
